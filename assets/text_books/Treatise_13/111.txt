Most common cause of nephrotic syndrome in adults may be diabetes mellitus.

Systemic lupus erythmatosus is the another important cause of nephrotic
syndrome.

Certain viral infection may cause this disease in both adults & children. The viral
infections include :

(i) Hepatitis ‘B’

(ii) Hepatitis 'C*

(ii) Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)

A number of drugs that are toxic to the kidneys can cause nephrotic syndrome as
a side effect. Following drugs are toxic to the kidney :

(i) Non steroidal anti inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)

(ii) Penicillamine

(iii) Heroin taken intravenously

(iv) Gold

The nephrotic syndrome may be caused by following types of allergies :
(i) Allergies to insect bites

(ii) Allergies to pollens

(iii) Allergies to poison ivy & poison oak

Some types of nephrotic syndromes are hereditary.

Acute nephrotic syndrome which develops into rapidly progressive
glomerulonephritis, most often, results from an abnormal immune reaction.

A very common cause of nephrotic syndrome is minimal change disease (MCD).

In this case there is minimal damage to the kidney. Many children with MCD
