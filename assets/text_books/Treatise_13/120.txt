40 | Kidney Failure or Renal Failure

Kidney failure is the inability of the kidneys to adequately filter the metabolic

waste products from the blood.

(1)

Kidney failure is of two types :
(1) Acute Renal Failure (ARF)
(2) Chronic Renal Failure (CRF)

Acute Renal Failure (ARF)

It is a sudden loss of kidney fucntion, most often, due to severe illness or injury.
Acute kidney failure can be very serious.

Most people recover.

Normal kidney function is restored, when the cause disease has been treated.
Main Symptoms

(i) _ It causes a life threatening accumulation of fluids & wastes in the body. This
is called fluid retention, with swelling of feet & ankles or puffiness of face &

hands.
(ii) There is drastic reduction in the urine production; Sometimes, it completly
Stops.
(iii) Following other symptoms may also appear :
e Nausea
e — Vomiting
e — Pruritus
e — Rapid heart rate
e Breathlessness
e Loss of appetite
e — Swelling in legs & in whole body.

Fatigue
As acute kidney failure pre persists and metabolic waste products
accumulate in the body, a person may experience fatigue.
