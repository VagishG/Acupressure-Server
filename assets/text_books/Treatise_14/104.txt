In presbyopia the light rays from a near object reach the retina before they can be
focused (as in farsightedness).

In presbyopia the ageing lens becomes rigid & stiff and can not be adjusted by the
ciliary muscles to bend the rays more sharply to move the focal point forward to fall on
the retina.

In farsightedness the eyeball is elongated or lens or cornea are excessively bending
the light rays.
