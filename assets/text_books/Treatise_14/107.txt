Scleritis (eas vee WATE)

Scleritis is inflammation of the sclera, the white of the eye.
Episcleritis is inflammation of the episclera.

Episclera is the transparent tissue that covers the sclera. It lies under the eyes outer
most layer, the conjunctiva.

It sometimes occurs with an autoimmune disease, such as rheumatoid arthritis or
inflammatory bowel disease.

Episcleritis is a milder inflammation. It affects young adults.
These disorders may affect one or both eyes.
Symptoms qe :

A violet red patch or small lump on the white of the eye.

The blood vessels in the eye may be inflammed & red.
There may be aching pain in the eyes.

It may cause some loss of vision or blurred vision.
Untreated scleritis may cause perforation in the eye's tissue.

Treatment options :

Anti inflammatory drugs in the form of eye drop or pill form are prescribed to reduce
the inflammation. Corticosteroid may also be needed.

Sometimes it has to be treated with immuno-suppresent drugs.
If perforation has occured, surgery may be needed to repair it.
