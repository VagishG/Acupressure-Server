This main risk of failure of the corneal transplant is rejection of the new cornea. But
this is rare. The cornea has no blood vessels, so antibodies are not formed & the
immune system cells can not get to the graft. But if blood vessels have grown into a
diseased cornea, the outlook is not so good.

Can the entire eye be transplanted ?

A whole eye can not be transplanted. The reason is that the optic nerve can not be

reconnected once it is severed. Because of this, the eye is never removed from its
socket during surgery.
