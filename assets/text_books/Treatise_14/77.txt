Farsightedness (axqftem)

Farsightedness is a condition in which objects at a distance are seen clearly. Nearby
objects are not seen clearly.

In farsightedness, the light rays from close objects are focused behind the retina, due
to a shortened eyeball.

People who have farsightedness may have headaches or eyestrain when reading.
This is due to the overworking of the ciliary muscle in trying to correct the problem
through accommodation.
