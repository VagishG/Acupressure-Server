In falciparum malaria, the infected red blood cells often stick to the walls of small blood
vessels and clog them. It results in damage to the brain (cerebral malaria), lungs and
kidneys.

Cerebral malaria is particularly dangerous complication that can produce high fever,

headache, drowsiness, delirium, confusion, seizure and coma. It most commonly occurs
in infants or young children, pregnant women and people who travel to high risk areas.

In falciparum malaria fluid can accumulate in the lungs & cause severe breathing
problems. Damage to multiple and cause a fall in blood pressure.

Black water fever is caused by the rupture of large numbers of red blood cells which
releases blood pigment into the blood stream. The released hemoglobin is excreted in the
urine, which makes the urine dark kidney damage may be severe rquiring dialysis. Black
water fever is more likely to develop in people who have taken quinine for treatment.
