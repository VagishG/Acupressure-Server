Heatstroke occurs because the body can not lose heat rapidly enough in extreme

heat. When the body can not cool itself, body temperature continues to rise rapidly to dan-
gerous high levels.

Heatstroke can temporarily or permanently damage vital organs, such as the heart,
lungs, kidneys, liver and brain. Death may occur.

Heatstroke may develop slowly over hours to days or quickly.
