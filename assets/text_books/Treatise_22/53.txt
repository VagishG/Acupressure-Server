Scleroderma

Life threatening complications can result when scleroderma affects the blood
vessels in the kidneys, it can cause exceptionally high blood pressure and kidney
damage.

Respiratory problems which are very common in diffuse scleroderma, cause
shortness of breath and increased susceptibility to pneumonia.
Gastrointestinal problems such as severe heartburn, bloating, diarrhea and
constipation, frequently occur.

The patient may also have difficulty in swallowing due to stiffness of the esophagus.
