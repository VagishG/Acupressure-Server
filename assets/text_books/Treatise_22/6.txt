Rheumatic Arthritis or Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD)
There are two stages of rheumatic heart disease :

i) Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF)

ii) | Chronic Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD)

i) Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF) :

Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF) usually affects children most commonly between 5
and 15 years or young adults.

Rheumatic fever is inflammation of the body's organ system, especially the joints and
the heart, resulting from the complication of streptococcal infection of the heart.

Rheumatic fever follows a streptococcal infection. It is not an infection. It is an
inflammatory reaction to the infection.

Most people with rheumatic fever recover, the heart is permanently damaged in a
small percentage of people.

Overcrowded living condition seem to increase the risk of rheumatic fever. Heredity
seems to play a part.

About half of the children who previously had rheumatic fever will develop it again with
another streptococcal throat infection.
