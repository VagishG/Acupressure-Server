ii) Chronic Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD) :

Rheumatic fever may permanently damage the heart valves, Resulting in Heart
Diseases(RHD).

In Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD), the valve between the left atrium and ventricle
(mitral valve) is most commonly damaged.

The mitral valve may become jerky {Mitral Valve Regurgitation (MR)}.
The mitral valve may become narrow {Mitral Valve Stenosis (M. S.)} or both.

Valve damage causes the characteristics heart murmurs that enable a doctor to
diagnose rheumatic fever.

Later in life, usually the middle age, the valve damage may cause heart failure and
atrial fibrillation, an abnormal heart rhythm.

If the heart valves become damaged, the risk of developing a valve infection
(endocarditis) remains throughout the life.

Those who have heart damage must always take an antibiotic before undergoing any
surgery, including dental surgery, even in adulthood.
